# Page Content Analysis: Customer Perspective

Many pages have **few sections and little content**. This doc identifies which pages feel thin, why it hurts the customer experience, and how to improve them.

## Why thin pages hurt (customer perspective)

1. **Trust** — Few sections = “Is this all?” Customers need proof, clarity, and next steps.
2. **Intent** — Someone on Case Studies wants outcomes; someone on Integrations wants “will it work with my stack?” Thin content doesn’t answer that.
3. **Conversion** — Signup and Blog have almost no reason-to-act (benefits, social proof, guarantees).
4. **SEO & bounce** — Very short pages can feel incomplete and increase bounce; a bit more structure and content helps.

## Recommended improvements (by page)

### 1. Case Studies (high impact)

**Today:** Hero + 3 cards. “Read case study” links to `#`.

**Add:**

- **Short intro block** — “Real teams, real results. See how businesses like yours improved ROAS and cut waste.”
- **Filters or tags** — By industry (Travel, E-commerce, Tech) or outcome (ROAS, CPA, time saved) so visitors find “someone like me.”
- **One highlighted case** — One expanded story (problem → what we did → result in numbers) so the page isn’t only cards.
- **CTA section** — “Get results like these” with Book Demo + Start Free Trial; optional testimonial line.
- **Fix links** — Point “Read case study” to real URLs (e.g. `/case-studies/travelcorp`) or remove until you have detail pages.

**Customer benefit:** “I see proof for my industry and a clear next step.”

---

### 2. Integrations (high impact)

**Today:** Hero + category grid + “Don’t see your platform?” CTA.

**Add:**

- **Why one platform** — One section: “Stop switching tabs. One login, one dashboard, one AI across Google, Meta, and OTAs.” (2–3 sentences.)
- **How connecting works** — “Connect in minutes: OAuth with Google/Meta/OTAs. No CSV imports. Data syncs every 15 minutes.” (reduces “will this be painful?”)
- **API / power users** — One line or small block: “Professional and Enterprise plans include REST API for custom integrations (CRM, BI, internal tools).” Link to docs or contact.
- **Bottom CTA band** — Same style as other pages: “Ready to connect your stack?” with Start Free Trial + Book Demo.

**Customer benefit:** “I know it works with my tools and how easy it is to connect.”

---

### 3. Signup (high impact)

**Today:** Hero + form only.

**Add (above or beside the form):**

- **What you get** — 3–4 bullets: “Full access to all 5 modules,” “Connect Google, Meta, OTAs,” “14-day free trial,” “No credit card required.”
- **Trust line** — “Join 2,000+ businesses” or “$5.2B+ in ad spend managed” (reuse from homepage).
- **Optional:** “Already have an account? Log in” link to login.

**Customer benefit:** “I know what I’m signing up for and that it’s low-risk.”

---

### 4. Knowledge Base (medium impact)

**Today:** Hero + category grid. All article links go to `#`.

**Add:**

- **Search** — Simple search input (even if it’s client-side filter by title/category) so “I have one question” visitors don’t have to scan everything.
- **Popular or Getting started** — “Start here” or “Most read” (e.g. Quick start, Connect Google Ads, First campaign) so new users have a path.
- **Can’t find it?** — Small block: “Can’t find your answer? Check the FAQ or contact support” with links.

**Customer benefit:** “I can find my topic or know where to ask.”

---

### 5. Blog (medium impact)

**Today:** Hero + post grid + email subscribe. Post links → `#`.

**Add:**

- **Category filter** — Reuse existing categories (Product Updates, Advertising Tips, Case Studies, Industry Trends) as tabs or pills so visitors can narrow.
- **Featured post** — One “Featured” or “Latest” post with slightly larger card and excerpt so the page isn’t only a grid.
- **Fix links** — When you have real posts, use real URLs (e.g. `/blog/ai-budget-allocator-2`); until then, you could point to homepage or a “Coming soon” note.
- **Subscribe** — Keep; consider one line: “Weekly digest. No spam. Unsubscribe anytime.”

**Customer benefit:** “I can browse by topic and see what’s new.”

---

### 6. Contact (medium impact)

**Today:** Hero + form + contact info + FAQ link.

**Add:**

- **What happens next** — One short block: “We usually respond within 24 hours. For demo requests we’ll suggest a time that works for you.”
- **Optional:** “Prefer to talk? Book a demo” link to `/book-demo`.

**Customer benefit:** “I know what to expect after I submit.”

---

### 7. Security (lower priority)

**Today:** Hero + 4 cards (encryption, SOC 2, infrastructure, access).

**Add:**

- **Report a vulnerability** — “If you’ve found a security issue, please report it to security@…” (email or link). Builds trust.
- **Short CTA** — “Questions about security? Contact us” or “See our compliance” if you have a PDF.

**Customer benefit:** “I see how you protect data and how to report issues.”

---

### 8. Legal (Privacy, Terms, Cookies) — optional

**Today:** Hero + long legal text. Fine as-is for compliance.

**Optional:**

- **One-line summary** at the top: “In short: we use your data to run the product, we don’t sell it, and you can request deletion. Details below.”

**Customer benefit:** “I get the gist without reading the full policy.”

## Quick wins (same pattern across pages)

- **Add a clear CTA section** at the bottom of every thin page: one headline, one line of copy, Book Demo + Start Free Trial (or Contact where it fits).
- **Reuse trust** — “$5.2B+ ad spend,” “2,000+ businesses,” “14-day trial, no credit card” on Signup, Integrations, Case Studies.
- **Fix placeholder links** — Replace `#` with real routes or a “Coming soon” state so customers aren’t left on a dead end.
- **One “Why this page?” line** — Under the hero on every secondary page: one sentence that answers “Why am I here?” (e.g. Integrations: “See every platform we support and how to connect in minutes.”)

---

## Implementation note

- Prefer **reusable components** (e.g. a `SectionCTA.astro` with headline, text, two buttons) so each page stays consistent and easy to update.
- Keep **tone and structure** aligned with Pricing Intelligence and the homepage: benefit-led, scannable, one main CTA per section.
